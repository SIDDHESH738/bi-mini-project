Project Overview

AI Impact on Jobs: A Comprehensive Data Mining and Business Intelligence Analysis (2010-2025)

This comprehensive Business Intelligence project analyzes the impact of Artificial Intelligence on the global job market from 2010 to 2025. Using advanced data mining techniques including classification and clustering, we investigate patterns in AI adoption, job displacement risks, automation trends, and salary dynamics across different industries, regions, and job roles.

Abstract

The study employs Random Forest Classification to predict job displacement risk levels and K-Means Clustering to identify distinct job market segments. Through rigorous preprocessing, feature engineering, and visualization techniques, we provide actionable insights for workforce planning, reskilling initiatives, and strategic decision-making in the AI era.

Key Research Objectives

  • Analyze temporal trends in AI adoption across industries and regions (2010-2025)
  • Identify job roles most susceptible to AI-driven displacement
  • Classify jobs based on automation risk and displacement probability
  • Discover natural clusters in the job market based on AI characteristics
  • Examine salary dynamics in relation to AI skills and automation risk
  • Provide actionable BI insights for strategic workforce planning

Methodology Overview

This study employs a multi-faceted data mining approach: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), Classification using Random Forest models, Clustering using K-Means algorithm, Interactive dashboards, and Business Intelligence synthesis.
